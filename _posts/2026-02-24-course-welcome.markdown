Welcome to **Agentic Large Language Models** at LIACS, Leiden University!

## About This Course

This course explores agentic LLMs — LLMs that reason, act, and interact — following our course textbook *Agentic Large Language Models*, written by the teaching team: Max van Duijn, Michiel van der Meer, Aske Plaat, and Niki van Stein. Lectures start **Tuesday, September 1, 2026**.

## What to Expect

Throughout the course, you will:

- Learn the fundamentals of Large Language Models: tokenization, transformers, training pipelines, and scaling laws
- Study advanced reasoning techniques: chain-of-thought prompting, self-reflection, and world models
- Build your own agentic LLMs across four hands-on case studies, from training a model from scratch to running a multi-agent social simulation
- Master tool use, retrieval augmentation (RAG), and the Model Context Protocol (MCP)
- Explore multi-agent systems and the behavioral and cognitive perspectives on agentic LLMs
